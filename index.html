🔍 How to Verify a Vendor's COA
1
Check Lab Accreditation
The testing lab must be ISO/IEC 17025:2017-accredited. You can verify accreditation directly on the lab's website. In-house "COAs" with no named lab are a red flag.
2
Verify Report ID
Every legitimate COA has a unique Report ID or batch number. Enter it on the testing lab's verification portal — if it doesn't resolve, the COA may be recycled or fake.
3
Check the Date
A COA more than 12–18 months old for your current batch is a warning sign. Vendors recycle old COAs to avoid retesting. The lot number on your vial must match exactly.
4
Look for HPLC Chromatogram
A real COA includes an HPLC chromatogram showing the purity peak profile, plus mass spectrometry molecular weight confirmation. Numbers-only reports without graphs are insufficient.
5
Injectable Peptides Need More
For anything injected (SubQ or IM), the COA must also include endotoxin (LPS) testing and sterility testing. Endotoxins cause fever, sepsis, and organ damage even at trace levels.
6
Confirm USA Origin
Ask the vendor directly where synthesis occurs. US Customs intercepted 5,000+ mis-labeled peptide shipments from China in 2025. "Tested in USA" ≠ "Made in USA."
